1. AI in Threat Detection and Response Systems

Threat detection and response systems leverage AI to monitor and analyze network traffic. Tools like Darktrace identify anomalies in real-time, flagging suspicious activities such as unauthorized access attempts or unexpected data transfers.
Example: During the WannaCry ransomware attack, AI systems successfully detected unusual activities, isolating affected areas and preventing further damage.

2. AI in Financial Fraud Detection

AI is revolutionizing fraud prevention in the finance sector. For instance, Mastercard uses AI to analyze millions of transactions instantly, detecting fraudulent patterns.
Example: Anomalies, like purchases occurring in multiple countries within minutes, are flagged, enabling real-time prevention of fraudulent activities.

Challenges in Application Contexts

1. Challenges in Threat Detection

  • False Positives: Legitimate activities flagged as threats can disrupt operations.
  • Adversarial AI: Hackers develop sophisticated attacks that evade detection.
  • Resource Intensive: Regular updates and retraining are costly but necessary.

2. Challenges in Fraud Detection

  • Privacy Concerns: Extensive data collection raises ethical questions.
  • Implementation Costs: Small businesses face barriers in adopting AI solutions.
  • Fraud Evolution: Cybercriminals continually improve their tactics, necessitating constant advancements.
